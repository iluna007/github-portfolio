About Me

I was trained as an architect, where my interest in code first emerged through computational design at IAAC, as a way of thinking through form, systems, and relationships beyond static representation. This interest deepened through spatial analysis and research practices at Centre for Research Architecture and through collaborative work with Forensic Architecture , where code, data, and interfaces became tools for investigation and evidence. Over the past two years, I have been working as a full-stack developer, building research-driven web platforms that combine front-end development, data visualization, and spatial thinking to turn complex information into clear, usable digital experiences. In parallel, I work as a visiting lecturer at the School of Architecture, University of Costa Rica .
